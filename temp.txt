<p><i><b>War to End All Wars</b></i> is the thirteenth studio album by guitarist <a href="/wiki/Yngwie_Malmsteen" title="Yngwie Malmsteen">Yngwie Malmsteen</a>, released on 7 November 2000 through <a href="/wiki/Pony_Canyon" title="Pony Canyon">Pony Canyon</a> (Japan), <a href="/wiki/Spitfire_Records" title="Spitfire Records">Spitfire Records</a> (United States) and DreamCatcher Records (Europe). While on tour for this album, singer <a href="/wiki/Mark_Boals" title="Mark Boals">Mark Boals</a> left the band and was replaced for a brief period by <a href="/wiki/J%C3%B8rn_Lande" title="Jørn Lande">Jørn Lande</a>; however, Boals returned to finish the tour. The <a href="/wiki/Instrumental" title="Instrumental">instrumental</a> song "Molto Arpeggiosa" is often mislabelled as "Arpeggios from Hell". The phrase "<a href="/wiki/The_war_to_end_war" title="The war to end war">the war to end all wars</a>" was historically used as a description of <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, especially in the period from 1918 to 1939.
</p>
